KYIV

ABOUT

Kyiv is a capital and most populous city of Ukraine. It located in north-central Ukraine along the Dnipro River. It was found in 482. Now it is home to many high-tech industries, higher education institutions, and historical landmarks.The city has an extensive system of public transport and infrastructure, including the Kyiv Metro.

KHRESHCHATYK

is the main street of Kyiv. The street is 1.2 kilometres long, and runs in a northeast-southwest direction from European Square through the Maidan to Bessarabska Square. The offices of the Kyiv City Council, the Post Office, the Ministry of Agrarian Policy and Food, the State Committee of Television and Radio Broadcasting, the TsUM department store, and the Ukrainian House, are all located on Khreshchatyk. Khreshchatyk is a popular attraction for visitors to the city and residents. During weekends, the street is closed to road traffic. It is a traditional setting for outdoor concerts and festivals, and is frequented by street musicians.

ANDRIIVSKYI DESCENT

is a historic descent connecting Kyiv's Upper Town district and the historically commercial center - Podil. The street is old part of the city and a major tourist attraction.

PEOPLES' FRIENDSHIP ARCH

is a monument in Kyiv. It was opened on 7 November 1982, amidst the celebration of the 1,500th Anniversary of Kyiv and the 60th Anniversary of the USSR.
